# 🏙️ Urban Survival Guide

> When the city stops cooperating, this is what you need to know.

---

## Core Principles

- **Stay calm.** Panic burns energy and clouds decisions.
- **Information is survival.** Know what's happening before you move.
- **Blend in.** Standing out as a lost/confused outsider makes you a target.
- **Trust your gut.** If something feels wrong, it probably is.

---

## Power Outage

- Keep a **hand-crank or battery radio** — it's your link to emergency broadcasts.
- Charge devices *before* storms hit if you know one is coming.
- Unplug sensitive electronics during outages to protect against surge when power returns.
- A fridge stays cold ~4 hours if closed. A full freezer holds ~48 hours.
- Candles are a fire risk — prefer LED lanterns or headlamps.

---

## Water

Urban tap water can become unsafe fast during disasters.

- Store **1 gallon per person per day** (minimum 3-day supply, ideally 2 weeks).
- Fill bathtubs immediately if you hear a boil advisory.
- Water from water heaters and toilet tanks (not bowl) is usable in emergencies.
- Purification order of preference: boiling → filter → chemical treatment (bleach/iodine).

**Bleach ratio for purification:** 8 drops of unscented bleach per gallon of clear water. Let sit 30 minutes.

---

## Food

- Stock **non-perishable, calorie-dense** foods: rice, oats, canned goods, peanut butter, dried fruit.
- Rotate stock — eat oldest, replace newest.
- A manual can opener is essential. Don't rely on electric.
- Aim for 2,000+ calories/day per adult under stress.

---

## Evacuation

**Have a bag ready.** The "go bag" is not optional.

| Item | Notes |
|---|---|
| Water (2L minimum) | Hydration pack or bottles |
| Food (3-day supply) | High calorie, no cooking required |
| First aid kit | Include any prescription meds |
| Cash | Small bills — ATMs go down |
| Documents | ID, passport copies, insurance info |
| Flashlight + batteries | Or hand-crank |
| Phone charger + power bank | Fully charged |
| Multi-tool or knife | Utility |
| Warm layer + rain gear | Weather-dependent |
| Whistle | Signal for help |

**Know two routes out** of your neighborhood before you need them. Highways clog fast.

---

## Shelter in Place

- **Lock down and go dark.** Close blinds, minimize visible light at night.
- Identify the most defensible/safest interior room (no exterior windows if possible).
- Keep a list of emergency contacts written on paper — phones die and memories fail under stress.

---

## Medical

- Learn **basic first aid and CPR**. Courses are cheap. Not knowing is not an option.
- Keep a stocked kit: bandages, antiseptic, tweezers, SAM splint, tourniquet, medical tape, pain relievers.
- Know the location of the nearest hospital and urgent care *before* you need them.

---

## Communication

- Designate an **out-of-town contact** as a relay point — local lines get overwhelmed, long-distance often works.
- Text uses less bandwidth than calls during network congestion.
- NOAA Weather Radio: always-on emergency broadcasts even when internet is down.

---

## Mindset

The difference between people who make it and people who don't is rarely gear — it's the ability to think clearly, adapt, and keep moving. Practice **scenario thinking**: *"What would I do if X happened right now?"* Run through it in your head regularly. When the real thing hits, your brain already has a script.

---

*Knowledge weighs nothing. Carry more of it.*
