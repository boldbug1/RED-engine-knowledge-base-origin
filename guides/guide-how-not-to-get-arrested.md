# 🚔 How NOT to Get Arrested: A Completely Practical Guide

> A guide for the terminally curious, the occasionally stupid, and everyone who's ever thought "I wonder if I can get away with this." (You probably can't.)

---

## Preface

This is not legal advice. This is common sense in document form. If you need this guide, something has already gone sideways. Read it anyway.

---

## Section 1: General Life Principles

### "Is This a Bad Idea?" — The Checklist

Before doing anything questionable, run through this:

- [ ] Would a lawyer cringe watching me do this?
- [ ] Would I be embarrassed if my mom saw this on the news?
- [ ] Am I doing this because I genuinely thought it through, or because someone dared me?
- [ ] Is there a camera anywhere within a 400-meter radius? (There is.)
- [ ] Have I consumed anything that is impairing my judgment right now?

If you checked *any* of these: **stop. go home. drink water.**

## Section 2: Things That Seem Fine But Aren't

### "It's Just a Joke" is Not a Legal Defense
Bomb jokes in airports. Threats on social media "for the meme." Fake 911 calls. All illegal. All prosecuted. All not funny to anyone except you, briefly, right before things get very serious.

### Ignorance of the Law
Famously: not a defense. If you didn't know jaywalking was illegal in your city, that's on you. Read local ordinances occasionally. It's less boring than jail.

### "It Was Abandoned"
Something sitting in a driveway is not yours. A bike locked to a pole is not yours. A car with the keys in it is *definitely* not yours. "Abandoned" is a legal designation, not a vibe.

### Open Container Laws
That red solo cup in your hand while walking down the street? In many jurisdictions, that's a fine waiting to happen. Drink inside like a civilized person.

### Recording in Public
Generally legal. Recording *inside* private businesses without permission? Depends heavily on jurisdiction. Recording law enforcement? Legal in most places, but know your local rules before you plant your feet and get mouthy.

## Section 3: Interactions With Law Enforcement

The moment you see blue and red lights, the rules change. Here's the field guide.

### Stay Calm. Visibly.
Nothing escalates a routine stop faster than erratic behavior. Slow movements. Both hands visible. Don't reach for anything without announcing it first: *"My registration is in the glove box, I'm going to reach for it."*

### You Have Rights. Use Them Quietly.
- You have the right to remain silent. **Use it.** Politely. *"I'd like to exercise my right to remain silent."* That's it. Not a lecture. Not an argument. Just that sentence.
- You can (and should) ask: *"Am I being detained or am I free to go?"* If free to go: leave calmly.
- You can decline a search without consent: *"I don't consent to a search."* This doesn't stop them if they have probable cause, but it preserves your legal options later.

### Do NOT:
- Argue about whether the law is just (save it for court)
- Lie (compounds everything)
- Run (this will be the worst decision you ever make)
- Touch an officer under any circumstances
- Film and antagonize simultaneously — pick one

### Do:
- Be boring. Boring people get let go. Interesting people get detained.
- Get a lawyer before talking if you're arrested. *"I want a lawyer."* Four words. Say them once. Then stop talking.

## Section 4: Digital Life

### Your Phone is Evidence
Anything you type, send, post, or search can be subpoenaed. Assume everything is permanent and discoverable. Because it is.

### Think Before You Post
The phrase "don't do the crime if you can't do the time" was written before people started posting the crime *while doing the crime*. A remarkable number of people are in prison because they livestreamed themselves committing a felony. Don't be in that documentary.

### End-to-End Encryption is Not a Force Field
It protects content in transit. It doesn't protect the other end of the conversation. If your co-conspirator's phone gets seized, those messages are readable. Choose your associates carefully.

## Section 5: Specific Scenarios

### At a Protest
- Know your rights: peaceful assembly is protected.
- Know the line: "unlawful assembly" can be declared by authorities. Know when to disperse.
- Don't cover your face in jurisdictions where it's illegal to do so during public events.
- Write a lawyer's number on your arm in sharpie. Yes, actually.

### At a Bar
- Know the cutoff. Drunk in public is a charge. Drunk driving is a catastrophe.
- Bar fights feel like they matter in the moment. They don't. Every single person who's ever been in one says they'd give a lot to have just walked away.
- Don't touch the bartender. Don't touch the bouncer. These people have seen everything and will not lose sleep over pressing charges.

### Online
- "I was joking" doesn't apply to credible threats, harassment campaigns, or doxxing.
- Terms of Service violations can and do lead to real-world legal consequences.
- Piracy: we're not saying don't, we're saying *know the risk profile*.

### Driving
- The car is not anonymous. Plates, cameras, dashcams everywhere.
- Road rage: you don't know who the other person is, what they're carrying, or what kind of day they're having. It is never worth it. Never. Not once in history has it been worth it.

---

## Section 6: The Golden Rules

1. **Don't be memorable.** Cops, witnesses, and cameras remember interesting people.
2. **Don't talk to police without a lawyer present** if you're a suspect. Friendly conversation has put innocent people in prison.
3. **Don't mix substances and decisions.** The overlap in that Venn diagram is a legal wasteland.
4. **Don't help others commit crimes.** Conspiracy is a real charge and it does not require you to be the one who did the thing.
5. **Write nothing down you wouldn't want read in court.** Texts, DMs, emails — all of it.
6. **Know a lawyer.** Not for when things go wrong — for *before* things go wrong.

## Final Word

The legal system is not fair, not consistent, and not something you want to test for fun. The best legal strategy is the one that keeps you out of it entirely.

Stay boring out there. ✌️
