# 🌍 Starter: Essential Human Knowledge

> The fundamentals that every human depends on, every single day — and mostly takes for granted.

---

## 🔥 Fire

Fire was the first technology. It still runs the world.

**What fire needs (the fire triangle):**
- **Heat** — ignition source
- **Fuel** — something to burn
- **Oxygen** — air to sustain it

Remove any one of these: fire dies. This is also how you fight it.

**How to start a fire:**
1. Prepare tinder first (dry grass, paper, cotton, bark shavings)
2. Build a small teepee of kindling (thin sticks) over it
3. Apply heat — lighter, match, or ferrocerium rod
4. Feed it slowly as it grows — too much fuel too fast smothers it
5. Add larger wood once you have a stable flame

**Fire gives you:**
- Warmth (core body temperature above 35°C / 95°F is survival)
- Cooked food (kills pathogens, unlocks more calories)
- Purified water (boiling)
- Light
- Protection (most animals avoid fire)
- Morale

**Fire safety:**
- Keep a clear perimeter — fire spreads in seconds
- Never leave unattended
- Smother with dirt or water, never leave just "burned out"
- Carbon monoxide (CO) is invisible, odorless, and lethal — never burn fuel indoors without ventilation

## 💧 Water

You can survive weeks without food. Days without water. Hours in extreme heat.

**Why water matters:**
- Your body is ~60% water
- Blood, digestion, temperature regulation, brain function — all water-dependent
- 2% dehydration = noticeable impairment. 10% = medical emergency.

**Daily need:** ~2–3 liters per person under normal conditions. More in heat or physical exertion.

**Finding water:**
- Follow terrain downhill — water collects in valleys
- Green vegetation clusters often indicate underground water
- Morning dew can be collected from surfaces
- Rain is generally safe to collect directly

**Making water safe to drink:**
- **Boiling** — 1 full minute rolling boil kills everything biological. Most reliable method.
- **Filtration** — removes particles and many pathogens (use proper filters, not just cloth alone)
- **Chemical treatment** — iodine tablets or unscented bleach (8 drops per liter of clear water)
- **Sunlight (SODIS)** — clear bottle, full direct sun, 6+ hours. Last resort.

**Never drink:**
- Saltwater (accelerates dehydration)
- Stagnant water without treatment
- Water near industrial or agricultural runoff without advanced filtration

**Store it:** Keep at minimum 3 days supply at home (1 gallon / ~4 liters per person per day).

## 🏠 Shelter

The human body is vulnerable. Shelter is what stands between you and the elements.

**What shelter protects against:**
- Hypothermia — body temperature dropping dangerously low
- Hyperthermia — overheating and heatstroke
- Wind, rain, snow, sun exposure
- Insects, animals

**Core concept:** Insulation — trapping air to slow heat exchange between your body and the environment.

**Elements of good shelter:**
- **Waterproof roof** — wet = cold 25x faster than dry
- **Ground insulation** — the ground pulls heat from your body rapidly
- **Wind blocking** — wind chill dramatically amplifies cold
- **Appropriate size** — smaller spaces retain body heat better

**At home:**
- Know where your water shutoff, gas shutoff, and electrical breaker are
- A well-insulated home stays habitable longer during power outages
- Identify the safest room in your home for different emergencies (tornado, intruder, fire)

**Emergency shelter hierarchy:**
1. Existing structure (building, vehicle)
2. Tarp or emergency blanket
3. Natural materials (debris hut, lean-to)

## 🍎 Food

Food is fuel, medicine, and culture. Understanding it at a basic level changes how you live.

**What your body actually needs:**

| Macronutrient | Role | Found in |
|---|---|---|
| Carbohydrates | Primary energy source | Grains, fruit, vegetables, sugar |
| Protein | Tissue repair, enzymes, immune function | Meat, legumes, eggs, dairy, nuts |
| Fat | Long-term energy, brain, hormones | Oils, nuts, meat, avocado |

| Micronutrient | Why it matters |
|---|---|
| Vitamin C | Immune function, collagen (deficiency = scurvy) |
| Vitamin D | Bone health, immunity (from sun or food) |
| Iron | Oxygen transport (deficiency = anemia) |
| Calcium | Bones, muscle function |
| Potassium | Heart rhythm, muscle function |
| B12 | Nerve function, red blood cells (critical for vegans) |

**Caloric baseline:** ~2,000 calories/day for an average adult at rest. More under physical stress.

**Food safety:**
- Bacteria multiply between 4°C and 60°C (40°F–140°F) — the "danger zone"
- Keep cold food cold. Keep hot food hot.
- When in doubt: throw it out. Food poisoning incapacitates you.
- Wash hands before handling food. This prevents most foodborne illness.

**Long-term storage staples:**
Rice, lentils, dried beans, oats, pasta, canned goods, honey (never expires), salt, oil.

**Growing your own:**
Even a small container garden can produce meaningful food — herbs, tomatoes, greens. Basic gardening knowledge: sun, soil quality, water, and drainage.

## 👕 Clothing

Clothing is portable shelter. It regulates your temperature and protects your skin.

**Layering system (the right way to dress):**

```
Base layer → Mid layer → Outer layer
(moisture)   (insulation)  (wind/rain)
```

- **Base layer:** Sits against your skin. Wicks moisture away. Merino wool or synthetic — not cotton (cotton kills in cold/wet; it holds moisture and stops insulating).
- **Mid layer:** Traps warm air. Fleece, down, or wool.
- **Outer layer:** Blocks wind and rain. Breathable waterproof shell.

**Heat loss from the body:**
- Head and neck lose heat rapidly — cover them in cold weather
- Wet clothing loses ~90% of its insulating value
- Hands and feet: extremities lose circulation first in cold — protect them early

**Footwear:**
One of the most important investments in daily life and survival. Proper footwear prevents blisters, injuries, and hypothermia. Wet feet in cold environments are a medical emergency over hours.

**Minimal wardrobe that covers most situations:**
- Moisture-wicking base layers
- Warm mid layer (fleece or wool)
- Waterproof jacket
- Durable pants
- Sturdy boots
- Hat and gloves (in any temperate or cold environment)

## 🌱 Basic Growing Knowledge

Every human for most of history knew how to grow food. This knowledge largely vanished in two generations.

**Seed to harvest — the basics:**
1. **Soil** — good soil is alive. It needs organic matter (compost), drainage, and appropriate pH.
2. **Seeds** — plant at the right depth (rule of thumb: 2–3x the seed's diameter)
3. **Water** — consistent moisture, especially at germination. Most plants want water at roots, not leaves.
4. **Sun** — most vegetables need 6–8 hours of direct sun
5. **Time** — respect growing seasons. Check your climate zone.

**Easiest plants to start with:** Tomatoes, lettuce, radishes, herbs (basil, mint), green beans.

**Composting:** Kitchen scraps + yard waste = free fertilizer. Requires: carbon material (dry leaves, cardboard) + nitrogen material (food scraps, grass) + moisture + occasional turning.

## 🧭 Navigation

Before GPS, humans navigated by landmarks, stars, and sun. These still work.

**Basic orientation:**
- Sun rises in the **east**, sets in the **west** — always
- Midday sun is **south** (northern hemisphere) or **north** (southern hemisphere)
- **Polaris (North Star):** Find the Big Dipper, follow the two pointer stars at the end of the cup. Polaris is directly true north from anywhere in the northern hemisphere.

**If lost:**
1. **Stop** — don't wander further
2. **Think** — retrace your steps mentally
3. **Signal** — three of anything is a universal distress signal
4. **Stay put** — rescuers search where you were last seen

**Compass basics:**
- Red needle points to magnetic north (slightly different from true north depending on location)
- Orient your map to north before reading it
- Pick a landmark in your direction of travel — walk to it, pick the next one

## 🧠 The Mental Foundation

All the physical knowledge above means less without the mental framework to use it.

- **Stay calm under pressure.** Panic narrows thinking. Practice pausing before reacting.
- **Prioritize correctly.** Shelter before water before food before everything else when survival is at stake. Oxygen before bleeding before shock in first aid.
- **Know what you don't know.** Overconfidence in survival situations kills people. "I think this plant is edible" is a dangerous sentence.
- **Rest is not optional.** Sleep deprivation impairs judgment as severely as alcohol. In any sustained challenge, protecting sleep protects decision-making.
- **Community matters.** Humans survived by cooperating, not individually. Know your neighbors. Build useful relationships before you need them.

## Quick Reference: When Everything Goes Wrong

```
Immediate threats to life:
1. Airway / Breathing
2. Bleeding
3. Body temperature (exposure)

Survival order of operations:
1. Safety (get out of danger)
2. Shelter / temperature regulation
3. Signal / call for help
4. Water
5. Food

At home — check these work before disaster:
□ Smoke + CO detectors (test monthly)
□ Fire extinguisher
□ First aid kit
□ 3-day water supply
□ 3-day food supply
□ Flashlight + batteries
□ Know how to shut off gas, water, and power
□ Emergency contact list on paper
```
