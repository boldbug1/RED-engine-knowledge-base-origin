# 🌲 Wilderness Survival Guide

> Nature doesn't care about your feelings. It does care about your decisions.

---

## The Rule of Threes

Survival priorities, in order:

| Threat | You have... |
|---|---|
| Severe psychological stress | 3 seconds to make a bad decision |
| Exposure (cold, heat) | ~3 hours without shelter |
| Water deprivation | ~3 days without water |
| Food deprivation | ~3 weeks without food |

**Shelter beats water beats food.** This order kills people who ignore it.

---

## Shelter

Building a shelter is your first task in cold or wet environments.

**Debris Hut (fast, effective):**
1. Find a long branch (ridgepole) ~9 feet long.
2. Prop one end on a stump or fork about 2.5 feet high.
3. Lean branches along both sides like ribs.
4. Pile on leaves, pine needles, bark — a foot thick minimum.
5. Crawl inside. Your body heat does the rest.

**Key rule:** The smaller the interior, the warmer it stays. Build it body-sized, not room-sized.

- Always insulate from the **ground** — ground sucks heat faster than cold air.
- Build on high ground to avoid flooding.
- Stay out of valleys and low areas in rain.

## Fire

Fire = warmth + water purification + signaling + morale.

**Fire triangle:** Fuel, oxygen, heat. All three or nothing.

**Tinder hierarchy:**
- Dry grass, birch bark, cattail fluff, dead pine needles
- Feather sticks (shaved wood curls)
- Cotton balls + petroleum jelly (prep these in advance)

**Starting fire:**
- **Lighter/matches:** Always carry. No heroics.
- **Ferrocerium rod:** Works wet, lasts thousands of strikes.
- **Bow drill:** Last resort. Takes practice and dry conditions.

**Fire lay types:**
- **Teepee:** Fast ignition, good for getting coals going.
- **Log cabin:** Long burn, good for sustained warmth.
- **Star fire:** Slow-feed long logs into center — conserves wood.

## Water

**Never drink untreated water** — Giardia and bacteria will incapacitate you worse than dehydration in the short term.

**Finding water:**
- Follow downhill terrain — water collects in valleys and low areas.
- Green vegetation and animal trails often lead to water.
- Morning dew can be collected from leaves with a cloth.
- Dig in dry riverbeds — water often flows underground.

**Purification methods:**
- **Boiling:** 1 minute rolling boil (3 minutes at high altitude). Most reliable.
- **Filter:** Improvise with layered cloth, charcoal, sand, and gravel — removes particulate but NOT pathogens alone.
- **Chemical tablets:** Iodine or chlorine. Effective, lightweight, pack them.
- **Solar disinfection (SODIS):** Clear bottle, full sun, 6+ hours. Last resort.

**Avoid:** Saltwater, urine, blood, alcohol, fuel-contaminated water. These accelerate dehydration.

## Food

You can survive weeks without food. Don't waste energy on it until shelter and water are secured.

**Foraging basics:**
- **Universal edibility test:** One part of one plant at a time, on skin first, then lips, then eat small amount and wait 8 hours.
- Avoid plants with milky sap, beans/seeds in pods, bitter/soapy taste, fine hairs.
- **Safe universal bets:** Dandelion (entire plant), cattail (roots, pollen), pine needles (Vitamin C tea), acorns (leach tannins first).

**Trapping:**
- Simple snares on game trails beat active hunting — they work while you sleep.
- Bait near water sources, tracks, and droppings.
- Check snares every few hours.

**Insects:** High protein, low energy to acquire. Avoid brightly colored ones. Remove legs and wings. Cook before eating.

## Navigation

**Always:**
- Tell someone where you're going and when to expect you back.
- Carry a map and compass. Know how to use them *before* you go out.

**Without tools:**
- **Sun:** Rises roughly east, sets roughly west. Midday sun is south (northern hemisphere).
- **Stars:** Polaris (North Star) sits directly over true north. Find it via the Big Dipper's pointer stars.
- **Moss:** Less reliable than popular belief — check multiple trees.
- **Follow water downstream** — it leads to civilization more often than not.

---

## Signaling for Rescue

**Rule:** Three of anything is a distress signal (3 whistle blasts, 3 fires in a triangle, 3 shots).

- **Fire and smoke:** Smoke visible for miles. Green leaves = white smoke, rubber/plastic = black smoke (daytime contrast).
- **Signal mirror:** Flash at aircraft and distant ridgelines.
- **Ground-to-air signals:** Stomp or mark large X in open clearing.
- **Whistle:** Carries farther than shouting and costs no energy.

**Stay put if possible** — searchers look for you where you were last seen. Moving makes you harder to find.

---

## Mindset

**STOP:**
- **S**top — don't keep moving blindly.
- **T**hink — assess your situation.
- **O**bserve — what resources do you have?
- **P**lan — choose a course of action.

Most wilderness deaths involve people who panicked and made rapid decisions. Slow down. Your brain is your most powerful survival tool.

*The wilderness doesn't hate you. It just doesn't know you're there. Act accordingly.*
